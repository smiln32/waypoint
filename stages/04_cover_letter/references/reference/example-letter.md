# Example Cover Letter

One finished, genuinely strong letter, shown in the app behind "See an example cover letter." It
models the editor's standards: opens on a requirement from the posting, every claim verifiable,
the award named, the inexperience stated plainly and answered. Edit this file to change the example —
the `letter` block is the letter; the bullets under "Why this letter works" appear beside it.

```letter
Dear Ms. Alvarez,

I am applying for the Operations Supervisor position at Northgate Logistics. Your posting asks for a supervisor who can run a 10-to-15 person shift while holding safety and accuracy standards — that was my daily work as a Marine Corps sergeant, and it is the work I want to keep doing.

For six years I led a team of up to 12 people, responsible for their performance, their safety, and more than $500,000 of equipment. We finished with 100 percent accountability — every audit clean, nothing lost — including a full overseas deployment. The Marine Corps recognized that work with the Navy and Marine Corps Achievement Medal, and the reasons come down to two things I would bring to your floor: my team met its deadlines, and the people on it got better. Several of my junior Marines were promoted on my watch.

I am new to the distribution industry, and I will not pretend otherwise. I will learn your systems the way I learned mine — quickly, and by respecting the people who already know them. What I bring on day one is a supervisor your team can count on.

I would welcome the chance to talk. Thank you for your time.

Sincerely,
James Carter
(555) 014-2876 · james.carter@example.com
```

## Why this letter works

- It opens by naming one requirement from the posting and matching it in the same sentence — the reader knows why they are reading by line two.
- Every claim is verifiable: team size, dollar value, audit record, a named award. Nothing is "known for" anything.
- The inexperience is stated plainly and answered with a plan — which reads as confidence, not weakness.
- It is short enough to read in under a minute, and every sentence earns its place.
