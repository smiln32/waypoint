# Example Cover Letter

One finished, genuinely strong letter, shown in the app behind "See an example cover letter." It
models the editor's standards: opens on a requirement from the posting, every claim verifiable
against the record, the inexperience stated plainly and answered. Edit this file to change the example —
the `letter` block is the letter; the bullets under "Why this letter works" appear beside it.

```letter
Dear Ms. Alvarez,

I am applying for the Maintenance Operations Supervisor position at Northgate Logistics. Your posting asks for someone who can keep a fleet running to schedule while holding safety and readiness standards across shifts — that was my daily work as a Marine Corps maintenance supervisor, and it is the work I want to keep doing.

For four years I ran maintenance control for a fleet of 12 tactical vehicles and led 18 Marines across two shifts. I owned the inspections, repair schedules, work orders, and parts requests, and I answered for equipment readiness every day — including a full overseas deployment, where downtime was not an option. Earlier I kept the maintenance records for more than 60 vehicles and pieces of support equipment. The through-line is simple: I keep equipment mission-capable, and I keep the standards clean under pressure.

I am new to commercial logistics, and I will not pretend otherwise. I will learn your systems the way I learned mine — quickly, and by respecting the people who already know them. What I bring on day one is a maintenance supervisor your team can count on.

I would welcome the chance to talk. Thank you for your time.

Sincerely,
James Carter
(555) 014-2876 · james.carter@example.com
```

## Why this letter works

- It opens by naming one requirement from the posting and matching it in the same sentence — the reader knows why they are reading by line two.
- Every claim is verifiable: shift size, fleet size, the responsibilities owned, the deployment. Nothing is "known for" anything.
- The inexperience is stated plainly and answered with a plan — which reads as confidence, not weakness.
- It is short enough to read in under a minute, and every sentence earns its place.
