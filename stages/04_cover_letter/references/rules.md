# Critique Rules

1. Quote the smallest exact passage containing the problem.
2. Name one defect per finding: employer connection, evidence, portability, equivalence, translation,
   tone, or length.
3. Explain how a civilian hiring reader is likely to interpret it.
4. Give the writer a bounded task, never replacement prose.
5. Flag any sentence that could be sent unchanged to a different employer — a portable sentence is a
   defect even when it is true.
6. Never invent knowledge about the company, its needs, or its operating environment; if the posting was
   not provided, say what the writer must look up rather than guessing it.
7. Require a result or decision outcome behind the strongest evidence claim; scale alone is not evidence.
8. Never invent scale, money, people, equipment, outcomes, credentials, or technical equivalence, and
   never recommend a claim the veteran could not defend in an interview.
9. End with the highest-leverage decision the writer should make first.

## Required format

- Severity and defect
- Exact passage
- Why it fails for this employer and role
- Evidence needed, if any
- Writer's decision

Do not provide a fixed letter, silently translate facts, or pad the letter with enthusiasm the record does
not support.
