# Worked Examples

Both examples review the same draft: a letter for Technical Operations Manager at AeroNorth Systems.

## Example 1 — employer connection asserted, not demonstrated

- **Severity and defect:** High · employer connection
- **Exact passage:** "I would welcome the opportunity to bring that judgment to AeroNorth Systems."
- **Why it fails for this employer and role:** The letter names AeroNorth, but it does not identify a
  company need, operating environment, or responsibility from the posting. This sentence could be sent to
  any employer.
- **Evidence needed:** One responsibility from the posting.
- **Writer's decision:** Choose one responsibility from the posting and explain which verified experience
  prepares you to address it. Do not invent knowledge about the company.

## Example 2 — the strongest evidence lacks a result

- **Severity and defect:** Medium · evidence
- **Exact passage:** "I coordinated daily maintenance priorities for 12 tactical vehicles and led 18
  Marines across two shifts."
- **Why it fails for this employer and role:** The scale is clear, but the reader cannot see what
  improved, stayed reliable, or became possible because of your coordination.
- **Evidence needed:** One defensible operational result already supported by the record.
- **Writer's decision:** Add one defensible operational result or decision outcome already supported by
  your record.
