# Cover Letter Templates

Templates supply the supporting language — complete sentences where only the writer's facts are
bracketed — plus a finished example to mirror. The substance stays the writer's: every bracket is a
fact they own and must be able to defend in an interview. Examples deliberately model what the
editors teach (military terms translated, titles honestly scoped, results with evidence).

The app loads these at render time: each `##` section is one template — the italic line is the
when-to-use description on its card, the `template` block is the skeleton loaded into the draft, and
the `example` block is the finished sample shown under "See a finished example."

## Answer the posting

_Best when you are responding to a specific job posting and can name one responsibility from it._

```template
Dear [hiring manager's name — check the posting or company page; if you can't find it, write "Hiring Manager"],

I am writing to apply for the [role title] position at [company]. Your posting asks for someone who can [copy one responsibility from the posting, in its own words]. That is the work I did for [number] years as a [your role, in civilian terms]: I [what you actually did — the people, systems, and scale involved, in plain language].

The result I am most prepared to repeat: [one specific outcome — what improved, stayed reliable, or became possible because of your work. A number helps, but only one you could defend in an interview].

I want to do this work at [company] because [one true reason — their operation, equipment, or mission. If you do not know enough about the company yet, research first; do not guess].

I would welcome the chance to talk about how I would approach [that same responsibility from paragraph one] in my first year. Thank you for your time.

Sincerely,
[Your name]
[Phone] · [Email]
```

```example
Dear Jordan Lee,

I am writing to apply for the Technical Operations Manager position at AeroNorth Systems. Your posting asks for someone who can coordinate maintenance planning across multiple technical teams. That is the work I did for four years as a maintenance control supervisor: I set the daily maintenance priorities for a fleet of 12 F/A-18 aircraft, directing 18 technicians across three shifts while tracking parts, open discrepancies, and release-to-service paperwork.

The result I am most prepared to repeat: through the busiest flying schedule of my tour, 96 percent of our aircraft were ready for their scheduled flights — a standard held by deciding, every morning, which jobs, parts, and people came first.

I want to do this work at AeroNorth because your fleet-support contracts run on the same daily tension I managed for years: safety, schedule, and parts availability pulling against each other.

I would welcome the chance to talk about how I would approach maintenance coordination in my first year. Thank you for your time.

Sincerely,
Alex Morgan
(555) 014-7821 · alex.morgan@example.com
```

## Translate your service

_Best when your experience is military and the reader is civilian — the letter does the translation for them._

```template
Dear [hiring manager's name, or "Hiring Manager"],

I am applying for the [role title] position at [company]. For [number] years I [your military work described in plain language — what you were responsible for and at what scale, with no unit names, acronyms, or rank].

In civilian terms, that meant [the translation: the decisions you made, the deadlines you balanced, the standard you were held to — written so a reader with no military background can picture the job]. The closest parallel to [company]'s work is [the specific overlap — the environment, the systems, or the stakes], where I [one verified example with an observable result].

What does not translate directly, I am closing deliberately: [the honest gap — a certification, tool, or industry vocabulary you are actively addressing] — [what you are doing about it right now].

I would welcome the chance to walk through how that experience applies to [one concrete part of this role].

Sincerely,
[Your name]
[Phone] · [Email]
```

```example
Dear Hiring Manager,

I am applying for the Maintenance Planning Lead position at Harbor Aviation. For eight years I maintained military jet aircraft and then ran the daily plan for their upkeep — first as the technician diagnosing radar and electrical faults, then as the supervisor deciding what got fixed, in what order, with which people and parts.

In civilian terms, that meant running the production schedule for a 12-aircraft fleet: balancing inspection deadlines, unscheduled repairs, and parts delays so aircraft were ready when the flying schedule needed them, under documentation standards where a signature certifies the aircraft is safe. The closest parallel to Harbor's repair-station work is that daily planning rhythm — intake, prioritize, assign, verify — which I held through four years of high-tempo operations.

What does not translate directly, I am closing deliberately: FAA repair-station terminology. I am working through the Part 145 regulations now so the vocabulary matches the experience behind it.

I would welcome the chance to walk through how that planning experience applies to your production schedule.

Sincerely,
Alex Morgan
(555) 014-7821 · alex.morgan@example.com
```

## Start from a referral

_Best when a person connected you to the role — name them early and earn the rest._

```template
Dear [hiring manager's name],

[Referrer's name], [how they know you and how they connect to the company — one clause], suggested I write to you about the [role title] position. When [referrer's first name] described [the specific problem or work the team is focused on], I recognized it: I spent [number] years [the experience that maps to that problem, in civilian terms].

[One verified example of handling that kind of problem — your specific action and the observable result. The referral opens the door; this paragraph has to hold it open on its own.]

[Referrer's first name] can speak to [what they have actually seen you do — keep this inside what they truly know]. The rest I would welcome the chance to discuss directly.

Sincerely,
[Your name]
[Phone] · [Email]
```

```example
Dear Priya Shah,

Marcus Webb, who served with me at MCAS Beaufort and now works on your infrastructure team, suggested I write to you about the Cybersecurity Operations Analyst position. When Marcus described the work — monitoring operational networks where an outage has physical consequences — I recognized it: I spent eight years in aviation maintenance, including a collateral assignment running information security for my 120-person company.

In that assignment I managed user access, ran the vulnerability scans on our maintenance network, and wrote the incident-response procedures we drilled twice a year — the same fundamentals, applied where a compromised system could ground aircraft. I hold a current CompTIA Security+ certification and am preparing for the SIEM certification your posting lists.

Marcus can speak to how I ran maintenance control under pressure. The security work I would welcome the chance to walk through directly.

Sincerely,
Alex Morgan
(555) 014-7821 · alex.morgan@example.com
```
