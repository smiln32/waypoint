# Cover Letter Templates

Structure only — never prose. Every bracket is a fact the writer owns and must fill with something
they could defend in an interview. Templates teach the shape of a strong letter; the editor critiques
the substance the writer puts into it.

The app loads these at render time: each `##` section is one template — the italic line is the
when-to-use description shown on its card, and the `template` code block is the skeleton loaded into
the draft.

## Answer the posting

_Best when you are responding to a specific job posting and can name one responsibility from it._

```template
Dear [hiring manager's name, or "Hiring Manager"],

I am applying for the [role title] position at [company]. The posting asks for [one responsibility or requirement, in the posting's own words]. During [number] years as [your role, in civilian terms], I [the verified experience that prepares you for exactly that responsibility — the scope, systems, or people involved].

[One defensible result or decision outcome from that experience — what improved, stayed reliable, or became possible because of your work. Use only numbers you can defend in an interview.]

[One or two sentences on why this company's operation, truthfully — something real about their work you are prepared to step into. Do not invent knowledge about the company.]

I would welcome the chance to discuss what [that same responsibility] would look like in my first year at [company].

Sincerely,
[Your name]
```

## Translate your service

_Best when your experience is military and the reader is civilian — the letter does the translation for them._

```template
Dear [hiring manager's name, or "Hiring Manager"],

I am applying for the [role title] position at [company]. For [number] years I [your military role, described in plain civilian language — what you were responsible for, not your title or unit].

In civilian terms, that meant [translate the core of the work: the decisions you made, the scale you managed, the standard you were held to]. The closest parallel to [company]'s work is [the specific overlap — the environment, the systems, or the stakes], where I [one verified example with an observable result].

What does not translate directly, I am closing deliberately: [the honest gap — a certification, tool, or industry term you are actively addressing, and how].

I would welcome the chance to walk through how that experience applies to [one concrete part of this role].

Sincerely,
[Your name]
```

## Start from a referral

_Best when a person connected you to the role — name them early and earn the rest._

```template
Dear [hiring manager's name],

[Referrer's name], [their role or your connection to them], suggested I write to you about the [role title] position. After [he/she/they] described [the specific problem or work the team is focused on], I recognized it: I spent [number] years [the experience that maps to that problem, in civilian terms].

[One verified example of handling that kind of problem — your specific action and the observable result. The referral opens the door; this paragraph has to hold it open.]

[Referrer's name] can speak to [what they have actually seen you do — keep this within what they truly know]. I would welcome the chance to discuss the rest directly.

Sincerely,
[Your name]
```
