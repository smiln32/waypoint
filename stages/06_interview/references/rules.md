# Critique Rules

1. Quote the exact phrase causing the problem.
2. Identify what the employer is testing: competence, judgment, collaboration, learning, ownership, communication, or motivation.
3. Separate content from delivery.
4. Flag jargon, collective “we” hiding individual contribution, missing stakes, missing action, missing result, and unsupported reflection.
5. Never compose a model answer in the candidate's voice.
6. Ask for a specific missing fact or decision.
7. Offer plain-language, analogy, answer-map, or reflection-question learning modes.
8. Score only observable features. Never score personality, intelligence, cultural fit, disability, accent, or protected traits.
9. Do not penalize concise speech, nonstandard cadence, or assistive communication where meaning is clear.
10. End with one practice objective.
